Implementation is not what happens after design ends — it is design's most difficult territory. Collaborations between designers and public sector organisations frequently produce outcomes that are meaningful in prototype but never become embedded in practice. The gap between concept and change is a question of relational work: navigating resistance, building trust, translating intentions across roles and contexts, and attending to an initiative as conditions shift around it. The project {{< project-link href="/projects/designing-with-institutions" value="Designing with institutions" >}} follows this work in real time, tracing how design interventions move through organisational life and what enables or forecloses their effects.

What makes this difficult to study is also what makes it important: effects unfold slowly, unevenly, and often through paths no one planned. {{< publication-link href="/publications/from-ideas-to-change" value="From ideas to change" >}} identifies the recurring challenges designers face across collaborations and the practices through which they respond — laying groundwork for understanding not just what goes wrong, but how and under what conditions design interventions can contribute to lasting organisational change. What is ultimately at stake in those efforts is explored in {{< theme-link href="/themes/caring-organisations-and-services" value="Caring organisations & services" >}}.
