Design and public administration have developed distinct practices and vocabularies when thinking about and aiming for change. When they meet in the context of complex societal challenges—climate change, housing shortages, social welfare, organised crime and many more—the encounter seems promising and generative but also unstable.


A central question that remains is what happens to that repertoire as it travels to the political-administrative domain—and whether design's claimed transformative potential holds when it enters these contexts, or whether it tends to reproduce the institutional logics it sets out to challenge. How that repertoire gets carried through the messy reality of implementation is what {{< theme-link href="/themes/stewarding-change" value="Stewarding change" >}} is about.
